What is the main difference between plant and animal cells?

(A) Animal cells are eukaryotes and plant cells are not

(B) Plant cells are eukaryotes and animal cells are not

(C) Animals cells have cell walls and plant cells do not

(D) ** Plant cells have cell walls and animal cells do not

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A prominent difference between a plant and an animal cell is the presence of a cell wall and chloroplasts. Plant cells have chloroplasts and the cell membrane has an outer covering called cell wall. Animal cells lack both chloroplasts and cell walls.

Concept note-2: -In plants, the cell wall surrounds the cell membrane. This gives the plant cell its unique rectangular shape. Animal cells simply have a cell membrane, but no cell wall.

Concept note-3: -A plant cell has a square or rectangular shape while the animal cells have an irregular or round shape.

Concept note-4: -Plant cell needs cell wall whereas animal cell do not because the plants need rigid structure so that they can grow up and out . All cells have cell membranes, and the membranes are flexible. So animal cells can have various shapes, but plant cells only have the shapes of their cell walls.
